You Survived the Rocky Mountains!

Lewis and Clark struggled through brutal snow, steep cliffs, and freezing winds while crossing the Rocky Mountains. The terrain was so rough that horses collapsed, supplies were lost, and every step forward drained the expedition's strength.

Food became dangerously scarce, forcing the men to ration carefully and survive on whatever they could find. Many were weak from hunger and exhaustion, and the freezing temperatures made traveling even harder. At night, they had to build shelters and fires quickly or risk frostbite or death.

The expedition also relied heavily on Native tribes for guidance and survival knowledge. Without their help, Lewis and Clark could have become lost in the endless mountain valleys. Crossing the Rockies became one of the most difficult and life-threatening parts of their journey west.
