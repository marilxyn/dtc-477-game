As Lewis and Clark traveled up the Missouri River, the journey became physically demanding and unpredictable. The river's strong currents, shifting sandbars, and hidden obstacles made travel slow and dangerous, often forcing the crew to push or pull their boats upstream by hand. They faced extreme weather, from intense heat to sudden storms, and had to rely on teamwork to keep moving forward. Along the way, they met Native American tribes who provided guidance, supplies, and important knowledge of the land. This part of the journey tested their endurance and problem-solving skills as they pushed deeper into unfamiliar territory.
