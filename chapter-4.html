Chapter 4: Grizzly Country

Lewis and Clark are journaling in the Montana wilderness.

The forest is silent... too silent.
Something heavy moves through the brush.

A grizzly steps into the open.

What should Lewis and Clark do?

You Escaped the Grizzly!

Lewis later wrote that grizzlies were far more dangerous than expected. Some bears charged repeatedly, and rifles sometimes failed to stop them quickly.

They Did Not Survive...

Standing your ground against a charging grizzly was extremely dangerous. Lewis wrote that grizzlies were far more powerful than they expected.
