What should Lewis and Clark do?

You Escaped the Grizzly!

Lewis and Clark's encounters with grizzly bears marked some of the most intense moments of their journey through the northern wilderness. Unlike anything they had previously faced, these animals influenced how the expedition moved through the landscape, often forcing longer, more cautious travel routes.

In their journals, they noted how grizzlies could appear suddenly in thick brush and react with surprising strength and aggression. They described how grizzlies often charged unexpectedly and could survive multiple gunshot wounds. These encounters made it clear that survival in this region required constant awareness and careful decision-making while traveling through dense forests and river valleys in what is now Montana.

Despite the danger, these observations became important scientific records. Lewis and Clark documented grizzly behavior, habitat, and range. Their accounts revealed just how untamed and unpredictable the northern land truly was.

They Did Not Survive...

Standing your ground against a grizzly bear was extremely dangerous. Lewis wrote that grizzly bears were far more powerful than they expected.
